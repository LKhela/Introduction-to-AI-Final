LEPL Ilia State University — GPA Calculation Rules

1. At Ilia State University, GPA (Grade Point Average) is the sum of the products of the GP (Grade Point) earned in each course and that course's credits, divided by the total credits of all courses the student has completed:

GPA = Σ (GP of each course × credits of each course) / Σ (credits of each course)

2. GP (Grade Point) is the converted score of the grade received in a single course, on a 4-point scale.

3. Under the GP system, grades are divided into the following categories:

  • F — 0 to 50 points (Fail)
  • E — 51 to 60 points
  • D — 61 to 70 points
  • C — 71 to 80 points
  • B — 81 to 90 points
  • A — 91 to 95 points
  • A+ — 96 to 100 points

4. An E-category grade (51–60) is always fixed at 1.00 GP. D, C, B, and A category grades (61 to 95 points) increase evenly in steps of 1/12 (≈0.08) per point. An A+ grade (96–100) is fixed and equals 4.00 GP.

5. GPA is awarded for all academic programs at Ilia State University, except doctoral-level studies. GPA is also not awarded for certificate programs.

6. Ilia State University issues only the final GPA, shown in the diploma supplement. This does not apply to the one-cycle Medical Doctor program.

7. When the final GPA is calculated, fractions are rounded to the nearest tenth.

Note: The logic of keeping GP constant ("frozen") within the E and A+ ranges reflects that differences between scores within these ranges are not significant. For example, if a student is graded within the 51–60 point range, it does not matter exactly which score was received — any score in that range is considered equally low. Likewise, if a student is graded within the 96–100 point range, it does not matter exactly how many points were accumulated — in either case, the score is considered equally outstanding.
